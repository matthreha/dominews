DOMinews: The Monthly Newsletter of the Department of Medicine

7.23
Welcome to the Department of Medicine!
Welcome to our new DOM faculty members! There are 91 new faculty, with 73 going to UCSF Health, 13 to ZSFG, and 5 to the VA. Our faculty are a vital source of energy and innovation, and we are confident that they will take UCSF and our department to even greater heights. See the list of new faculty and orientation dates.

Honors and Awards
Kwame Adjepong received a Dean’s Diversity Fund Award and was named a John A. Watson Faculty Scholar.
Lynnea Mills was appointed Academy Chair for Innovation in Teaching.
Sirisha Narayana was appointed Academy Chair for Excellence in Foundational Teaching.

DOM Cohort Grant 
Monica Yang in the Division of Rheumatology at UCSF Health is this year’s awardee for the Department of Medicine’s Cohort Grant for 2023. Her project entitled “Molecular profiling across organ systems in Systemic Sclerosis: A longitudinal cohort study,” will receive $100,000 for a two-year period to expand the cohort.

Other Honors and Awards
Akemi Brown and Madison Malone received Mount Zion Campus Resident Awards for Excellence from the Mount Zion Health Fund (MZHF).

Mitch Feldman received a Fulbright Specialist Program Award to complete a project at the Faculty of Public Health at Mahidol University in Thailand.

James Frank received the Osler Distinguished Teaching Award from the UCSF School of Medicine.
Ashwin Kotwal received the Long-Term Mentor Award from the UCSF School of Medicine.

Tom Martin was awarded the Brian G.M. Durie Outstanding Achievement Award from the International Myeloma Foundation.

Larissa Thomas co-edited the book, Caring for Caregivers to Be.

Heather Whelan received the UCSF Sustainability Award for Faculty.

Introducing DOMinews!
Did you notice? This monthly DOM newsletter has been redesigned and renamed! You’ll still find all the same great content — honors and news, publications, grants, media appearances, and events — but with a refreshed and modern look. Tell us what you think!

DOM in the News
John Balmes was quoted on KCBS-AM advising pregnant women to avoid wildfire smoke.

Peter Chin-Hong wrote an op-ed for the Los Angeles Times explaining why Covid is still killing people despite medical advances. He was also quoted by ABC News saying that high wastewater rates of Covid virus don’t necessarily indicate that a surge is imminent and by NBC News weighing in on the Oregon Health Authority’s relaxation of Covid guidelines.

Meghana Gadgil spoke on the National Academies New Voices’ One Health webinar series about the threat that climate change poses to human health.

Dawn Gross was featured on the “A Beautiful Death” episode of the Tell Us Something We Don't Know podcast.

Alka Kanaya was quoted by NPR noting that Asian Americans may develop diabetes at lower BMIs and advocating for changes to screening guidelines.

Suneil Koliwad was interviewed by Wired emphasizing the need for new, non-invasive approaches to modulate hunger, satiety, and gut function.

Carla Kuon was quoted in the San Francisco Chronicle explaining her success in treating long Covid with naltrexone and on KCBS-AM discussing holistic approaches to treating long Covid.
Margot Kushel was quoted by the PBS News Hour  providing warning that the country is currently unprepared to deal with the growing numbers of aging unhoused people.

Rita Redberg was quoted in the New York Times expressing concern that the use of polygenic risk scores could label healthy, asymptomatic people as “patients.”

Sergio Torres was featured on the “Don't Just Do Something...Stand There” episode of the Dying to Talk podcast.

Hilary Seligman was interviewed by the Conversation asserting that a reduction in SNAP (Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program) benefits will increase food insecurity rates.

Alan Venook was quoted in the New York Times commenting on a study showing that rectal cancer patients did not benefit from radiation.

Flavio Vincenti was featured in the San Francisco Chronicle advocating for Bristol Myers to continue its research and for support of the immunosuppressant drug belatacept.

Bob Wachter was interviewed by the Leadership in Healthcare podcast of Sanford Health discussing healthcare's digital revolution. He also co-wrote an op-ed for STAT about the benefits and challenges of opportunistic CT scans.

Recent Grants of Note
Margot Kushel and team: a grant of $4.6M over 5 years from the National Institute on Aging for a project entitled, “Empowering Formerly Homeless Older Adults to Engage in Advance Care Planning in Permanent Supportive Housing (ACP-PSH): An RCT.””

Matthew Springer and team: a grant of $3.5M over 5 years from the National Institute on Drug Abuse for a project entitled, “Effects of chronic marijuana use on endothelial function.”

Midori Kato-Maeda and team: a grant of $3.3M over 5 years from the National Insititute of Allergy and Infectious Diseases for a project entitled, “Innovating anti-tuberculosis drug susceptibility testing with a novel and rapid non-culture based phenotypic test using MPT64 biomarker.”
